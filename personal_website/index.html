About Me

I am a graduate of Ramapo College of New Jersey, with majors in Computer Science and Bioinformatics.

Initially, when I entered college, I quickly picked up Bioinformatics for the interdisciplinarity and breadth of knowledge I would gain. To this, I added Computer Science to complement the skills I would acquire.

Since then, I have worked on a variety of bioinformatics research projects, including working with Dr. Paramjeet Bagga and Dr. Scott Frees to characterize the relationship between cis-regulatory elements in mammalian polyadenylation.

In 2015, I was selected as one of eight students to participate in an NSF-sponsored research internship with Rosetta Commons. Over the course of the internship, I learned to use and develop Rosetta, eventually presenting my research under Dr. Jens Meiler at RosettaCON in Seattle.

Over the course of many years, I have dipped my hands into many hobbies and interests, including classical history, law, literary theory, pen and paper role-playing games, game design, philology, card games, papercrafts, circuitry, music, and modeling kits.
